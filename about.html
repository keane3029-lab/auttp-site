Threat Briefing

THE THREAT

UTTP is not a one-off troll. It is a coordinated hate group that has been systematically targeting content creators for years — and platforms are still failing to stop them.

Who They Are

WHAT IS UTTP?

UTTP (also known as "Uptoppers") is an organized hate group that targets content creators — especially animators, artists, and small YouTubers — through coordinated mass false reporting, harassment raids, and channel takedown campaigns.

Their goal is simple: silence creators they disagree with by abusing automated platform systems. They exploit the fact that YouTube and other platforms rely heavily on automated strike systems that can't distinguish between legitimate reports and coordinated spam.

This isn't a new problem. UTTP has been operating for years, adapting their methods, recruiting new members, and continuing their campaigns even as individual accounts get banned. They always come back. That's why we need platforms to address them at the root — not just ban individual accounts on a case-by-case basis.

THREAT INDICATORS
  • Coordinated reporting from multiple accounts simultaneously
  • Targeting of small creators with fewer than 10K subscribers
  • Focus on animation, art, and fandom communities
  • Use of alternate accounts after main accounts are banned
  • Harassment campaigns in comments and community posts
  • Organized via private Discord servers and group chats
  • Patterns of activity across multiple platforms at once

How They Operate

THEIR TACTICS

01
Primary Tactic
MASS FALSE REPORTING

Groups of UTTP members file the same copyright or Community Guidelines report simultaneously on a target channel. YouTube's automated systems interpret volume as legitimacy — flagging channels even when the reports are completely fabricated, resulting in unearned strikes, removals, or terminations.

02
Secondary Tactic
HARASSMENT RAIDS

Organized raids on target channels involving coordinated toxic comments, hate speech, impersonation attempts, and mass dislike campaigns. The goal is to intimidate creators into deleting content, going private, or quitting entirely — without needing the formal report system at all.

03
Infiltration Tactic
COMMUNITY INFILTRATION

UTTP members create accounts that blend into creative communities to gather intel, manufacture drama, and spread false narratives about target creators before the reporting campaign begins. By the time the raids start, distrust and confusion have already been seeded in the community.

04
System Exploit
PLATFORM LOOPHOLE ABUSE

UTTP specifically exploit the gap between YouTube's automated enforcement and human review. They know that automated strikes can remove content or restrict channels within hours, while the appeals process takes days or weeks — meaning creators lose income and visibility even when they ultimately win the appeal.
